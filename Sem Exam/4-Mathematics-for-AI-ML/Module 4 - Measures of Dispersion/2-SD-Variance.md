## ğŸ“Œ 1. Variance ($\sigma^2$)
Average squared distance from the Mean.
$\sigma^2 = \frac{\sum (x - \mu)^2}{N}$ (Population)
$s^2 = \frac{\sum (x - \bar{x})^2}{n-1}$ (Sample)

## ğŸ“Œ 2. Standard Deviation (SD, $\sigma$)
Square root of Variance.
$\sigma = \sqrt{Variance}$
- **Why take root?** To return to the original units (e.g., from $cm^2$ back to $cm$).
- **Rule of Thumb**: For Normal Distribution:
  - 68% data within $\mu \pm 1\sigma$
  - 95% data within $\mu \pm 2\sigma$
  - 99.7% data within $\mu \pm 3\sigma$

## ğŸ“Œ 3. Coefficient of Variation (CV)
Relative measure of dispersion. Good for comparing two datasets with different units.
$CV = \frac{\sigma}{\mu} \times 100$
