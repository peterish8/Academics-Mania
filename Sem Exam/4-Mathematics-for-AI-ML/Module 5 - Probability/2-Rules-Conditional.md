From the conditional formula:
$P(A \cap B) = P(A|B) \times P(B)$
- **Independent Events**: If A doesn't affect B, then $P(A|B) = P(A)$.
  - $P(A \cap B) = P(A) \times P(B)$

The probability of A **OR** B happening.
$P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$
- **Mutually Exclusive**: If they can't happen together ($P(A \cap B) = 0$).
  - $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B)$
